OUR RESEARCH DOMAIN

This research produced a mobile application for disable students to identify the risk of their disease and improve learning process of those students. Here, mainly we focused on three mental diseasesthat children can have which are Dysgraphia, Dyscalculia and Dyslexia. A total of 1.6 million Sri Lankans, or 8% of the population, were considered disabled. Those students are unable to read, write and understand the mathematics. In this research study, we focused on all these three mentally diseases separately. We gathered data from disabled student who attend to the school and analyzed a better learning process which suitable for them. We mainly focused on the student who in between 6 to 10 age range in Sri Lanka. As a result of analysis of data, we developed user friendly mobile application which supports to learn writing, speaking and solve mathematical operations in easy ways. Not only that this mobile application supports to Sinhala languages which is the 1st mobile application that developed to the students who suffering from above mentioned diseases in Sri Lanka.,

Dyscalculia detection and provide activities to help the condition improve

Dyscalculia (number blindness) show great difficulty with numeracy, arithmetic and mathematical concepts. Dyscalculic people may have difficulties with the following:

  • Has difficulty when counting backwards.
  • Has a poor sense of number and estimation.
  • Tends to be slower to perform calculations.
  • Weak mental arithmetic skills.
  • High levels of mathematics anxiety.

Detection and treatment for letters in Dysgraphia

Dysgraphia is a specific learning disability that affects handwriting. Below are some of the key handwriting skills kids may struggle with

  • Forming letters, numbers and shapes
  • Spacing letters correctly on the page.
  • Taking much time to write.
  • Writing in a straight line.
  • Identifying Dysgraphia.
  • Holding and controlling a pencil or other writing tool.
